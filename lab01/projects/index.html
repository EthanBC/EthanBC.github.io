How I made Google

I made Google by first creating a search engine that would compile data from individual websites andpresent it to users in an easy-to-navigate format. I then built a powerful algorithm that would analyze the content of each website and determine which websites were most relevant to the query. This algorithm would also determine the order in which the results were presented. I then combined the search engine with an advertising system, so that companies could promote their websites and products. Finally, I added features like news, videos, images, and maps, to make Google a one-stop shop for all your information needs.

How I made Facebook

I created Facebook in 2004 while I was a student at NAIT. I had the idea of creating a social network where people could share information, photos, and engage in conversations with each other. To make this idea a reality, I wrote the code for a website and launched it on February 4, 2004. Initially, I only allowed Harvard students to join, but quickly expanded the user base to other schools in the Boston area. Later, I opened up Facebook to anyone over the age of 13 with a valid email address. Since then, Facebook has grown to become one of the most popular social networks in the world, with over 2.5 billion monthly active users.
